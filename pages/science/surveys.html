Galaxy Surveys

Our group has been involved in the design and build up of many galaxy surveys currently used in astronomy. The surveys are designed to address specific scientific questions including: deep surveys to study the evolution of galaxies (CANDELS), wide surveys to study the large-scale structures (COSMOS, COSMOS-Web), surveys with extensive multi-waveband photometry and spectroscopy to study the physical properties of galaxies (MOSDEF), galaxy clusters (BUFFALO), and the study of properties of galaxies at different redshifts and environments (Hawaii Two-O survey). We have been involved in observational programs (from the stage of writing proposals to observations, data reduction, and cataloging).

CANDELS

üîó Survey Website

With the installation of the WFC3, the accessible spectral range on the HST was extended to both shorter (UV) and longer (near-infrared) wavelengths. The Cosmic Assembly Near-infrared Deep Extragalactic Legacy Survey (CANDELS). With 900 orbits, this is the largest single project performed on the Hubble Space Telescope. The CANDELS was designed to study the evolution of galaxies to the re-ionization epoch (1 billion years after the Big Bang) and study galaxies at the peak of their star formation activity, detecting over 250,000 galaxies.

CANDELS carried out a search for SNe Type Ia at z~1.5. One of the highest redshift galaxies (GN-z11) was found in GOODS-N at a redshift of 11. The light received by us from this galaxy left the galaxy 400 million years after the Big Bang. The galaxy has 1% of the Milky Way's mass but was formed 20 times faster.

References
Grogin et al. 2011; Koekemoer et al. 2011

COSMOS

üîó Survey Website

Cosmic Evolution Survey (COSMOS) is a wide area survey performed with allocation of 600 HST orbits. It covers an area of 2 sq. deg in F850LP filter. Since its inception, the COSMOS has grown to be one of the most widely observed fields in extragalactic astronomy. It provides the widest contiguous survey of galaxies. The growth of the COSMOS can be seen in COSMOS2015, COSMOS2020, and COSMOS2025 catalogs. It now has over 40 filters (broad, medium-band, and narrow-band).

Reference
Scoville et al. 2007

MOSDEF

üîó Publications

Using the multi-object infrared spectrograph (MOSFIRE) on the Keck Telescope, we could perform spectroscopy targeting rest-frame optical features for high redshift galaxies. This provides diagnostic spectral lines indicative of physical conditions in galaxies. The MOSFIRE Deep Evolution Field (MOSDEF) is a collaboration between UCR, UCLA, UCB, and UCSD targeting a mass selected (H-band selected) galaxies at 1.5 < z < 3.5 in CANDELS fields. We have obtained spectral data for ~2000 galaxies.

BUFFALO

üîó Survey Website

Beyond Ultra-deep Frontier Fields And Legacy Observations (BUFFALO) is a survey built around the six Hubble Space Telescope (HST) Frontier Fields clusters designed to learn about early galactic assembly and clustering and prepare targets for observations with the James Webb Space Telescope. BUFFALO will place significant new constraints on how and when the most massive and luminous galaxies in the universe formed and how early galaxy formation is linked to dark matter assembly.

The BUFFALO photometric catalogs contain detailed information for over 100,000 astronomical sources in 6 clusters spanning a total of 240 arcmin¬≤ area. BUFFALO is an international collaboration of ~100 astronomers from 13 countries.

Hawaii-20 (H20)

üîó Survey Website

The Hawaii Two-0 Survey (H20) is a 20 deg¬≤ ultradeep galaxy survey combining Subaru Hyper-SuprimeCam (HSC) imaging (grizy), CFHT MegaCam imaging (u-band), Spitzer imaging (IRAC CH 1 & 2), and Keck DEIMOS spectroscopy of two Euclid Deep Calibration Fields (EDF-North and EDF-Fornax ‚Äì each 10 sq. deg area). We are using Subaru Prime Focus Spectrograph (PFS) to measure redshifts for ~250,000 galaxies in the H20 fields.

COSMOS-Web

üîó Survey Website

COSMOS-Web is a 255-hour treasury program on the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) covering a contiguous area of 0.5 sq. deg using NIRCam imaging survey in four filters (F115W, F150W, F277W, and F444W) reaching 5œÉ point source depth of 27.5‚Äì28.2 mag. The COSMOS-Web was designed to (1) identify thousands of galaxies in the re-ionization epoch (6 < z < 11) to study the spatial distribution of galaxies, (2) identify rare populations of galaxies including quiescent galaxies at z > 4, and (3) study the stellar-to-halo mass relation at z~2.

Reference

CEERS

üîó Survey Website

The Cosmic Evolution Early Release Science (CEERS) survey was designed to study the nature of first generation of galaxies 500 million years after the Big Bang. It targets galaxy formation from z~0.5 to z>10 using several imaging and spectroscopic modes. We use NIRCam imaging to survey 35.5 sq. arcmin area to search for galaxies at z > 9. The CEERS consists of NIRCam imaging, NIRCam grism, NIRSpec R~1000 and R~100, and MIRI imaging.
