Zahra Sattari

Postdoctoral Scholar

Caltech/IPAC

Zahra Sattari is a Postdoctoral Scholar at Caltech/IPAC who studies galaxy evolution across cosmic time. She uses near-infrared slitless spectroscopy from the James Webb Space Telescope to investigate star formation and chemical enrichment of galaxies, and how these properties are shaped by their large-scale environments. During her PhD at UC Riverside, she utilized Hubble Space Telescope imaging to investigate the properties of clumpy galaxies and conducted spectroscopic studies to examine metal enrichment in protocluster environments and low-mass systems.
